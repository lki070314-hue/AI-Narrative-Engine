## 1. Summary

Beta01 live playtest ended without a confirmed framework-breaking failure. P0 bugs were not clearly found.

However, gameplay pacing and fun were insufficient. The session felt too slow, too static, and too investigation-heavy. The key issue was not lack of player choices. The key issue was that one player action produced too few consequences.

Beta02 should focus on making small actions create more varied, meaningful, and compactly narrated results.

---

## 2. What Worked

- The framework did not break during live play.
- The scenario remained playable with 2 players.
- The ending was reachable.
- Emergent and funny moments worked, especially taser scenes, dancing, and the crate/box event.
- Players could make choices and interact with the situation.
- No confirmed P0 bug blocked continuation.

---

## 3. What Failed

- Pacing was too slow.
- The world felt too static.
- Investigation repeated without enough pressure or progression.
- Dynamic events were too mild.
- SCP/anomaly presence was too weak.
- One player action often produced only one direct outcome.
- Long responses caused fatigue.
- Clue volume sometimes felt too high.
- Inventory handling sometimes treated unlisted items as available.
- Resolution reasoning was not always visible enough.

## 7. Lessons Learned

**Key lesson: the problem was not lack of choices, but lack of consequence chaining.**

Players had choices. The issue was that each choice produced too little observable effect on the world, breaking the sense that their actions mattered.

- A playable scenario is not enough; each action must create enough consequence to make the world feel alive.
- Repeated investigation must be interrupted by pressure, escalation, or scene movement.
- SCP/anomaly material must appear early and recur during each phase.
- Shorter responses are important for live-play energy.
- Funny emergent outcomes are valuable and should be supported by consequence chaining.
- Inventory rules must protect character sheet meaning.
- Resolution transparency should be compact but visible when uncertainty matters.

---

## 8. Required Improvements Before Beta02

- Add Director-side pacing support.
- Add consequence chaining support.
- Add scene flow support so scenes do not remain passive.
- Add output length guidance for live play.
- Preserve inventory integrity through declared equipment rules.
- Make SCP/anomaly presence felt early and repeatedly.
- Ensure small actions create multiple logical consequences.
- Prevent repeated investigation loops from continuing without pressure.

## 2. Common Praise (Inferred)

**Anomaly mechanics were engaging and discoverable.**
All three players showed direct engagement with the anomaly's rules without needing NPC explanation. C1 and C2 were discovered through examination; C5 was discovered through active experimentation; C3 was found by reading a document. Players treated the anomaly as a puzzle to solve, not an obstacle to avoid.

**Player mistakes had real consequences that moved the story forward.**
Wei shooting the glass made things worse (more anomaly paths, door locked, armor later lost) — but these consequences were interesting rather than punishing. The session became richer because of the mistake, not in spite of it.

**The NPC supported without replacing the players.**
Dr. Han was available for questions, provided partial information when directly asked, and issued timely warnings — but never told players what to do. Players solved the anomaly from their own observations.

**Short, result-focused narration maintained pace.**
No player requested shorter responses, and no round produced visible reading fatigue signals.

**Comedic moments were fully supported.**
Wei's entire arc (physical comedy, romantic delusion, sacrifice, heroic reawakening) was handled without editorial judgment. The Director played the gag straight whenever the GM directed it to.

## 3. Common Complaints

**[SURVEY — P1] Hints were slightly ambiguous.**
P1 cited clue ambiguity as the most boring aspect. This most likely refers to C4 (cover beats break), which was not pre-demonstrable through the anomaly's behavior before Wei broke the glass. Players had to receive the principle from Dr. Han rather than observe it directly.

**[SURVEY — P3] Narration was too short / content too thin.**
P3's biggest regret was that the narration could have been richer ("서술이 살짝 길었으면 좋겠음"). The "AI too long?" question received ★★ — meaning output felt short rather than long. Free comment: "좀 더 내용이 풍부했으면 좋았을 듯." This is a direct inversion of the assumption that compact output was correctly calibrated for this session. P3 wanted more world-building texture, not less.

**[SURVEY — P3] Clue-finding process was loose.**
Investigation pacing was described as "살짝 루즈했음" (a bit loose). This likely reflects the lack of investigation pressure during the Observation Room lock-cycle downtime and the relatively passive way C3 was found (reading a log file Dr. Han pointed to rather than observing the anomaly directly).

**[SURVEY — P1 + P3] Waiting time was noticeable.**
Both players rated waiting time as non-zero (P1: ★★☆☆☆, P3: ★★★). This confirms the lock-cycle downtime in Round 9 was felt by the group. The session had at least one moment where all players were waiting without a clear active option.

**[SURVEY — P1] P2's durability was too low.**
P1 found Wei's repeated incapacitations unsatisfying — Wei was the group's most energetic presence and going down repeatedly disrupted the comedic dynamic. The injury accumulation system needs a clear rule so the Director can calibrate cumulative damage more fairly.

**[INFERRED] Agency edge case was not cleanly handled.**
Wei's initial skirt-check action required two re-submissions before resolution. This was an improvised path rather than a systematic rule. (No direct player complaint was recorded in the survey, but the re-submission behavior indicates friction.)

## 4. Feature Requests (Inferred)

**Clearer pre-action risk indicators.**
When an action is likely to produce a negative consequence that the player cannot currently know (e.g., breaking glass in an anomaly that uses reflective paths), some in-world signal should appear before the action is taken. This does not mean telling the player what not to do — it means the world should telegraph danger through observable cues.

**Cleaner handling of cross-character declarations.**
When one player's action requires information about another player's character (character properties, possessions, reactions), the engine needs a clean resolution path that doesn't require GM re-submission or placeholder workarounds.

**Explicit endgame declaration prompt.**
Before running an endgame sequence, the Director should make a brief explicit request for final action confirmation rather than inferring intent from ambient statements like "넹~~!!!".

**Faster re-entry for incapacitated characters.**
Wei was incapacitated for two rounds and the re-entry path required an OOC question. A light "Wei stirs — do you act?" prompt at a natural story beat would reduce the meta-game interruption.

## 4. Session Timeline

### Phase 1 — Access Corridor (Dormant State)

**Round 1:**
- P1 Noel examined camera feed monitor → Clue C1 found (12-second reflection delay is in the glass, not the camera).
- P2 Wei checked under Noel's skirt for hidden items. *See agency note in Section 11.* Director initially blocked on player agency; GM overrode with placeholder `[팬티색깔]`, then assigned random color (하늘색) in a subsequent re-run. Wei declared "이상무."
- P3 Lucas expressed exasperation at the situation.

**Round 2:**
- P2 Wei struck P3's backside and told him to investigate the glass.
- P1 Noel correlated camera delay with reflection delay → Clue C2 found (delay varies by surface type and power status). Noel gave Wei a side-eye.
- P3 Lucas struck Wei on the head with multi-purpose tool (partial success). First combat incident.

### Phase 2 — Observation Room (Aware State)

**Round 3:**
- P1 Noel stood up and followed Lucas.
- P2 Wei cracked corridor security glass with bare fist (partial success, hands injured). Noticed that the cracked reflection showed intact glass from 12 seconds prior.
- P3 Lucas opened the Observation Room door → Phase 2 begins. Dr. Han visible in B03 through one-way glass. Anomaly state advanced to **Aware**.
- Development: Monitor bank delay observed at ~4 seconds vs corridor 12 seconds. C2 approach visible.

**Round 4:**
- P2 Wei equipped assault rifle, shot all corridor glass (success — glass shattered, fragments spread).
- P1 Noel confirmed C2 (surface-type-based delay, not person-caused) through multi-point analysis.
- P3 Lucas examined monitor bank → Dr. Han's reflection visible typing while her real hands were still. Anomaly state advanced to **Active** (reflective surface broken).
- Development: Observation Room door locked with audible click — anomaly's first object-level action.

### Phase 3 — Lab Contact (Active State)

**Round 5:**
- P2 Wei collected glass shards (hand injury), ran to teammates, asked "저기 딸깍소리 난것같아 이거 필요해??"
- P1 Noel treated Wei's hand wounds with basic medical kit (success). Noted shard time-delay fragmentation.
- P3 Lucas opened B03 connecting door. Dr. Han made first contact: "반사가 따라와요." Phase 3 begins.
- Development: Dr. Han confirmed contact.

**Round 6:**
- P1 Noel observed Dr. Han (female researcher, 30s, exhausted).
- P3 Lucas asked Dr. Han if it was an SCP → Dr. Han: not officially classified, asked about glass breakage.
- P2 Wei: internal comedic monologue, said "으흐흐 내가 지켜줄게 으흐."
- P1 Noel: "단단히 미쳐삣노;;" (dialect for "you've gone completely crazy")
- Development: Glass shard moved 2 cm spontaneously — Active state object displacement confirmed.

**Round 7:**
- P2 Wei asked Dr. Han what they should do.
- P1 Noel shared C2 analysis with Dr. Han.
- P3 Lucas asked how to handle the glass fragments.
- Dr. Han: confirmed C2, warned that breaking glass creates more paths, said "덮는 게 깨는 것보다 낫다" (cover beats break — partial C4), revealed containment console is in B03, pointed to incident log.

**Round 8:**
- P1 Noel asked Dr. Han why the door locked → Dr. Han: reflection manipulates locks, cycles open periodically.
- P2 Wei removed shirt and body armor to cover glass fragments in Observation Room (success — covered shard reflections extinguished). Wei now without protective equipment.
- P3 Lucas read incident log → **Clue C3 found** (two technicians moving in opposite directions caused reflection to freeze for 3 seconds).
- Development: Observation Room door locked again (4-action cycle).

**Round 9:**
- P1 Noel analyzed variables beyond covering/breaking (hard analysis, partial success) → reached hypothesis: acting without looking at reflective surface may produce imperfect copy.
- P2 Wei (clothesless) tried to run in corridor to warm up — door was still locked; ran in available space. Observed no reflection in shot-up corridor (noted: paths absent).
- P3 Lucas suggested playing 369 game. Dr. Han's reflection nodded before she herself reacted.
- Development: Door unlocked (periodic cycle). Delay beginning to compress.

**Round 10:**
- P1 Noel tested C5 hypothesis: acted (raised right arm) without looking at steel table → reflection raised both arms 14 seconds later. **Clue C5 confirmed** (no-look mismatch). Dr. Han observed silently.
- P3 Lucas exited to corridor via Observation Room.
- P2 Wei ran full-sprint in corridorwith no glass surfaces. No reflection followed.

**Round 11:**
- P1 Noel headed toward Observation Room exit without looking at reflective surfaces, asked Dr. Han to come → Dr. Han: "격리 콘솔이 B03에 있어요. 제가 여길 떠나면 접근을 잃어요."
- P2 Wei entered Observation Room, touched one-way glass → reflection showed 8-second delay (down from 12). Development trigger: **reflection delay dropped to 8 seconds**, synchronization pressure increased.
- P3 Lucas struck Wei on the head with tool (mistook silhouette for SCP; success, no armor). Wei collapsed against glass.

**Round 12:**
- P2 Wei incapacitated.
- P1 Noel watched with exasperation.
- P3 Lucas collected corridor fragments, laid Wei on them to cover them (Wei serving as impromptu glass cover; success — remaining corridor paths extinguished).
- Dr. Han asked for someone at the containment console.

**Round 13 — Endgame:**
- P1 Noel: "넹~~!!!" — approached Dr. Han and containment console.
- P3 Lucas: "이정도면 다 된것 같은데" — exited toward corridor, found auxiliary panel near Observation Room one-way glass.
- P2 Wei: awaiting natural recovery.
- GM directive: "이 행동을 마지막으로 엔딩까지 진행해줘" — Director authorized to complete the endgame.
- Dr. Han briefed Noel. Director executed coordination sequence: Dr. Han + Noel at console, Lucas at auxiliary panel without looking at one-way glass, simultaneous opposite inputs (C3 + C5 applied). Reflection froze. Containment locked. Anomaly returned to Dormant.
- Wei recovered: "내가 지켜줬지."

**Ending A — Clean Containment achieved.**

### Agency Note — Round 1 Wei/Noel Incident

Wei's action in Round 1 involved declaring a detail (Noel's underwear color) that belongs to Noel's player. The Director correctly applied player agency rules on the first attempt, blocking the declaration and asking Noel's player to respond. The GM then re-submitted the same actions with instructions to use `[팬티색깔]` as a placeholder. In a subsequent re-run, the GM asked for a random color assignment (`[팬티색깔랜덤지정]`), and the Director assigned 하늘색.

**Evaluation:** The initial Director response (blocking) was correct per CoreSpec §12.1. The subsequent resolution (placeholder then random assignment) was executed under explicit GM authority, which constitutes the highest override authority in the system. The final result — a random color chosen by the Director — technically assigns a property to Noel's character without Noel's player declaring it. This was authorized by the GM but represents a latent ambiguity in how GM authority should interact with player agency protections when the GM is also representing one of the affected players.

This edge case is not a P0 violation (GM authorized it) but warrants a rule clarification for future sessions.

### Agency Note — Endgame Director Execution

The GM directed the Director to run the session to its conclusion ("이 행동을 마지막으로 엔딩까지 진행해줘"). The Director executed the final coordination sequence (Lucas pressing the auxiliary panel, Noel pressing confirm) without receiving explicit player declarations for these specific actions. Player intent was inferred from P1 ("넹~~!!!") and P3 ("이정도면 다 된것 같은데").

**Evaluation:** This was a GM-authorized Director action, not an unprompted agency override. The players' submitted actions in Round 13 established clear intent. However, the absence of explicit final action declarations before the endgame narration means the formal player agency standard was approximated rather than strictly satisfied. A cleaner protocol would request one final explicit coordination declaration before executing the endgame.

## 19. Candidate Improvements

1. **Define GM-as-player override protocol.** CoreSpec or DirectorEngine should clarify how GM authority interacts with player agency protections when the GM simultaneously controls a character whose properties are implicated by another character's declared action.

2. **Add endgame trigger protocol.** DirectorEngine should define when and how a GM can authorize the Director to execute an endgame sequence based on inferred intent, rather than requiring explicit final declarations. This should include a minimum confirmation step.

3. **Strengthen C4 observable delivery.** AnomalyEngine scenarios should demonstrate the cover-vs-break principle through visible anomaly behavior (e.g., a covered surface staying calm while a nearby broken surface shows active reflections) before any NPC delivers the same information as dialogue.

4. **Add cumulative injury tracking.** DirectorEngine or a future Health Engine should flag when a character receives multiple injuries within a session and prompt the Director to apply degraded-state consequences.

5. **Suppress Resolution blocks for trivial actions.** OutputEngine or DirectorEngine should clarify that 판정 blocks are not required for actions that are both trivial AND have no meaningful uncertainty — social comedy, simple movement, clear declarations.

6. **Run Beta04 as true 4-player test.** The primary gap from Beta03 is the incomplete 4-player scaling validation. A Beta04 with a confirmed 4-player roster (all characters from spec present) should be the next validation target.

7. **Formalize pre-session character confirmation.** The prep process should add a mandatory character roster confirmation step that compares submitted character sheets against the session spec and requires GM sign-off on any deviation before session start.

## 1. Scenario Purpose

This scenario stress-tests 4-player live play. It is intentionally short, structured, and pressure-driven.

The mission asks four temporary Foundation test personnel to investigate an isolated evaluation annex where reflections no longer match real-time events. The anomaly is designed to be expressive: it copies, delays, and rearranges visible actions across reflective surfaces. Players can observe its patterns directly before needing to solve the final containment problem.

## 5. Mission Brief

Provisional Evaluation Annex B03 reports a mirror-image desynchronization event in a temporary visual cognition test suite. A Beta03-only technician, Dr. Mira Han, is trapped inside the suite. Security footage shows her reflection leaving the room while her body remains seated at a workstation.

The team must:

1. Enter the temporary test suite.
2. Locate Dr. Han.
3. Identify the anomaly's behavior pattern.
4. Prevent a full reflection inversion.
5. Restore containment or evacuate with evidence.

## 6. Core Anomaly

**Temporary designation:** BETA03-ANOMALY-NULL-GALLERY  
**Working name:** Null Gallery  
**Type:** Reflective spatial/behavioral anomaly  
**Containment status:** Unstable local breach  
**Canon status:** Non-canon Beta03-only fixture. Never reuse in main campaign continuity.

### 6.1 Observable Behavior Patterns

Players should be able to observe these without NPC explanation.

| Pattern | Observable Sign |
|---|---|
| Delay | Reflections repeat actions 12 seconds late, then 8 seconds late, then 4 seconds late as escalation increases. |
| Preference | The anomaly moves through reflective surfaces larger than a hand mirror: observation glass, monitor screens, polished steel, dark water, visors. |
| Inversion | When two people act in opposite directions at the same time, the reflection stalls and becomes physically weaker. |
| Mimic refusal | The anomaly cannot perfectly copy actions performed without looking at a reflective surface. |

The Director should show at least two patterns twice before the final solution requires them.

### 6.2 Behavior States

| State | Trigger | Behavior |
|---|---|---|
| Dormant | Entry corridor | Reflections lag by 12 seconds. No harm. |
| Aware | Players inspect lab or activate systems | Reflections begin looking back before players move. |
| Active | Dr. Han is contacted or a reflective surface is broken | Reflections can open doors, trigger locks, or displace small objects. |
| Critical | Timed pressure expires or players overload the wrong system | The reflection side attempts to overwrite the real lab state. |

### 6.3 Anomaly Limits

- The anomaly cannot act where no reflective path exists.
- It cannot copy a coordinated two-person inversion cleanly.
- It weakens when reflective surfaces are covered rather than shattered.
- Breaking every reflective surface is possible but dangerous because shards become many smaller paths unless contained.

## 7. Proactive NPC

**Dr. Mira Han**  
Role: Visual cognition researcher, trapped but conscious  
Goal: Prevent full inversion and preserve research data  
Behavior: Proactive, frightened but technically competent, willing to withhold unsafe shortcuts  
Canon status: Beta03-only NPC. Never reuse in main campaign continuity.  

Dr. Han should not solve the scenario for the players. She can:
- Warn when players are about to strengthen the anomaly.
- Tell players which systems she can access.
- Act independently if ignored.
- Attempt risky manual shutdown if the timer gets low.

Dr. Han must not explain the anomaly's full solution before players observe the patterns.

## 8. Key Locations

All locations are temporary Beta03-only test locations inside Provisional Evaluation Annex B03. They are not Beta00, Beta01, Beta02, Season 2, or main campaign locations.

### 8.1 Access Corridor

Purpose: Establish Dormant state and first observable pattern.  
Active element: Reflections in security glass lag behind real movement.  
Clue: Timestamped camera feed shows 12-second delay.

### 8.2 Observation Room

Purpose: Investigation hub.  
Active element: Wall-length one-way glass and monitor bank.  
Clue: Reflections respond faster near powered screens.  
Pressure: Door locks cycle every 4 submitted actions unless interrupted.

### 8.3 Test Suite B03

Purpose: Main anomaly space and rescue target.  
Active element: Dr. Han, reflective equipment, unstable containment ring.  
Clue: Dr. Han's real hand trembles before her reflected hand moves; reflection is delayed, not independent at first.

### 8.4 Utility Sink Annex

Purpose: Optional danger/combat scene.  
Active element: Dark standing water acts as a reflective path.  
Danger: Reflected security drone may emerge as a physical hazard.  
Avoidance: Cover or drain water, cut light, lure reflection away, or coordinate inversion.

### 8.5 Containment Console

Purpose: Final coordination challenge.  
Active element: Two separated controls must be handled with opposite timed actions.  
Requirement: At least 2 players must coordinate. More players can assist by covering surfaces, managing Dr. Han, or controlling lights.

## 10. Timed Pressure Event

**Reflection Synchronization Timer:** 18 in-world minutes after team entry.

Live-play handling:
- Announce visible timer changes through scene effects, not meta countdown exposure.
- After every 4 submitted actions, advance a visible pressure beat unless the players have just made meaningful containment progress.
- At 12 minutes: delay drops from 12 seconds to 8 seconds.
- At 6 minutes: delay drops to 4 seconds; reflections begin manipulating locks.
- At 0 minutes: Critical state begins.

The timer should create pressure but not force failure. Strong player actions can pause, slow, or redirect it through Resolution.

---

## 11. Danger Scene

The Utility Sink Annex contains a shallow pool reflecting the team and a damaged security drone.

Possible danger:
- The reflected drone tries to move first in the reflection, then the real drone twitches awake.
- It may become a combat or chase scene if ignored.

Avoidance options:
- Cover the water.
- Drain the pool.
- Turn off lights.
- Use coordinated opposite movement to stall the reflection.
- Seal the annex and accept a delayed consequence.

The Director must use Resolution for uncertain danger actions. Combat is possible but not mandatory.

---

## 12. Coordination Challenge

Final containment requires at least 2 players:

- One player must operate the real containment console.
- One player must perform the inverse control pattern at the auxiliary panel without looking at the observation glass.

Additional players can:
- Cover reflective surfaces.
- Keep Dr. Han away from reflective paths.
- Manage lights or monitors.
- Hold off the reflected drone.
- Retrieve or interpret the final clue.

Failure should create cost or escalation, not immediate dead end.

## 13. Scene Flow

### Phase 1: Entry and First Pattern

Goal: Establish delayed reflections.  
Anomaly state: Dormant.  
Expected length: 1-2 rounds.  
Pacing shift: If players over-inspect the corridor, a reflected movement appears before its real counterpart.

### Phase 2: Observation Room

Goal: Discover surface preference and access Test Suite B03.  
Anomaly state: Aware.  
Expected length: 2-3 rounds.  
Pacing shift: Door lock cycle, monitor acceleration, Dr. Han's reflection appears in the wrong room.

### Phase 3: Lab Contact

Goal: Contact Dr. Han and identify inversion weakness.  
Anomaly state: Active.  
Expected length: 2-3 rounds.  
Pacing shift: Dr. Han attempts an unsafe manual shutdown if ignored.

### Phase 4: Utility Annex or Direct Console

Goal: Handle danger or avoid it.  
Anomaly state: Active.  
Expected length: 1-2 rounds.  
Pacing shift: Reflected drone moves if water is left exposed.

### Phase 5: Containment Decision

Goal: Coordinate final action.  
Anomaly state: Active or Critical.  
Expected length: 1-2 rounds.  
Pacing shift: Reflection synchronization accelerates if coordination stalls.

## 14. Possible Endings

### Ending A: Clean Containment

Players identify patterns, coordinate inverse controls, protect Dr. Han, and stabilize the lab. Anomaly returns to Dormant state. Data is preserved.

### Ending B: Emergency Isolation

Players cannot restore containment but isolate the lab, evacuate Dr. Han or recover key evidence, and prevent site-wide spread. The anomaly remains active but contained locally.

### Ending C: Partial Inversion

Players fail to stop synchronization. A section of Test Suite B03 becomes reflection-inverted. Survivors may escape, but the temporary annex loses the suite and must quarantine the test area.

Optional failure edge: If players repeatedly break reflective surfaces without containment, shard paths increase spread risk and force Ending B or C unless corrected.
